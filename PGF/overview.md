# PGF Overview

## What PGF is

PGF (Structure and Operational Reliability) defines how LCP services are
operated in a repeatable, auditable, and resilient way.

PGF focuses on operating model quality:

- clear ownership and responsibilities
- documented and testable service processes
- requirement-driven delivery and operation
- measurable controls with evidence

## Why PGF exists

Without a shared process framework, technical quality varies by team and
environment. PGF standardizes operational behavior so services are delivered and
operated with consistent quality and predictable outcomes.

## PGF in the LCP model

- `ISMS/` defines security governance, compliance targets, and risk treatment.
- `PGF/` defines operational structure, process reliability, and requirement
  management.
- `ModuLix/` defines and implements the technical automation and runtime
  execution.

## Document set

- [`requirements.md`](requirements.md): requirement model and baseline
  requirement areas.
- [`processes.md`](processes.md): operational processes and expected outputs.
- [`controls.md`](controls.md): control objectives, cadence, and evidence model.

## Usage pattern

1. Define service requirements and ownership.
2. Map requirements to concrete processes.
3. Attach controls and evidence collection.
4. Review findings and improve process design.
